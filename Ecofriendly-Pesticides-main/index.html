About the project

People plus crop, and livestock (animals and birds) production play essential roles in the social and economic systems of Egypt, but they are frequently subjected to the direct and indirect devastating effects of arthropod pests which adversely impacts health, productivity, economy, and welfare as they damage agricultural crops, consume and/or damage harvested food, or transmit diseases to humans, animals, and plants. The direct feeding activity of pests result in irritation, hypersensitivity, increased disturbance, reduced time grazing or ruminating, blood-loss, loss of weight gain, reduction in milk yield, loss of draught power, impacts on meat, wool, and hide and ultimately death. The past 30 years has witnessed dramatic emergence and re-emergence of epidemic vector-borne diseases throughout the world
